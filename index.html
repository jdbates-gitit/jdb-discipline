Tao Te Ching · Legge translation
Chapter 10

When the intelligent and animal souls are held together in one embrace, they can be kept from separating. When one gives undivided attention to the (vital) breath, and brings it to the utmost degree of pliancy, he can become as a (tender) babe. When he has cleansed away the most mysterious sights (of his imagination), he can become without a flaw. In loving the people and ruling the state, cannot he proceed without any (purpose of) action? In the opening and shutting of his gates of heaven, cannot he do so as a female bird? While his intelligence reaches in every direction, cannot he (appear to) be without knowledge? (The Tao) produces (all things) and nourishes them; it produces them and does not claim them as its own; it does all, and yet does not boast of it; it presides over all, and yet does not control them. This is what is called 'The mysterious Quality' (of the Tao).

What it's pointing at

This chapter teaches that wholeness comes from integration—holding our opposing natures together, breathing with suppleness, and releasing the mental noise that fragments us. True power flows not from force or claiming credit, but from acting naturally without rigid intention, like a mother nurturing without controlling. The Tao accomplishes everything while remaining humble and invisible, and we find peace by embodying that same receptive, unattached presence.

Read against today

We live in an age of spectacular fragmentation: divided against ourselves, against each other, constantly announcing our positions and demanding recognition. The chapter's invitation to become 'as a female bird'—responsive rather than dominating, open rather than fortified—mirrors what our exhausted culture most lacks. In a world of relentless claiming, controlling, and boasting, the quiet teaching that the greatest work asks nothing for itself feels almost radical. Our suffering multiplies not because problems are unsolvable, but because we approach them with clenched fists and inflated egos. The Tao suggests that what we most desperately need—healing, understanding, genuine change—requires us to release our grip.

To carry today

Today, notice where you are holding tight—to a story about yourself, a position you must defend, credit you need to claim. In one small moment, practice releasing that grip and see what becomes possible. Like the tender babe in the chapter, you need not know everything or control everything to be whole and safe.
